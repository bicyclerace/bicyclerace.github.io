Findings

Divvy Stations Around UIC

Six stations around UIC were selected for analysis, and these stations were compared to overall statistics for the city of Chicago between July and December 2013.

Gender Balance

The Divvy stations near UIC showed a striking difference in gender balance when compared to Chicago as a whole. The overall gender balance of Divvy riders in Chicago is heavily skewed towards men, with roughly four to five more male than female riders.

Near UIC, however, the gender balance of Divvy riders is almost equal, with roughly 5,000 female riders and roughly 7,000 male riders entering and leaving stations near UIC.

Age

As expected for a student population, the age of Divvy riders around UIC was generally lower than for the overall population of Chicago. For Chicago as a whole, the most common age of Divvy riders was around 30 years old for the time period examined. For riders near UIC, the age distribution was skewed, with the most common age being closer to 25 years old.

User Type

The relative distribution of user type for Divvy riders near UIC was nearly identical to the distribution of user types for Chicago as a whole. This was a bit surprising, as one might expect more students to be Divvy subscribers, as opposed to one-time Divvy customers.

Events in the City

Beginning of School

The 2013 fall semester began on August 26. The overall city traffic on the first day of school was compared to traffic one week before. While the overall distribution of rides by time of day was not drastically different on the first day of school, there was a noticeable increase of 21-to-30 year old traffic around 8 am.
