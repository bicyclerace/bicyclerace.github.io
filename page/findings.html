General Patterns observed in the data

On December 1st, there is a fair amount of activity, more or less typical of what you might come to expect. However, on December 2nd, divvy bike usage drops to a near nothing, with very brief and small spikes of activity occurring periodically throughout the day. Less than 5 divvy bikes were utilized on that day

Bike usage compared to the current weather (temperature, rain, etc)

Intuitively as temperatures drop, so too does the number of trips per day. Further, there is a direct correlation between the temperate and time of day. While the temperature is low, but the sun is high, there tended to be more trips being taken. As the evening approached, the number of trips per hour dropped more dramatically, often with less than 10 trips an hour.

Bike usage compared to the current sunrise and sunset times

Across the board bike activity was high during daylight hours, meanwhile bike usage dropped as it grew darker outside.
