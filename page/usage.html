Usage

Viewing Overall Station Popularity

Station popularity can be visualized by selecting the "Popularity" icon at the bottom menu bar.

The level of popularity is described by a color gradient ranging from yellow to red, with red denoting the most popular station, while yellow identifies the least popular stations.

We have further defined popularity based on the daily in-flow and out-flow of bikes. That is, the number of bikes that passed in and out of the station. We chose this representation over comparing the total number of bikes a station has encountered due to the fact that certain stations are only active for short windows of time during the year, while others are active year-around. By showing the average daily inflow/outflow we allow for a more equal comparison across all stations without misrepresenting the level of activity of one station over the other.

Viewing Overall Statistics

There are three charts, each highlighting a different type of information about the divvy bike data.

Divvy rider demographics

The first of these charts (in the top-left corner) focuses on the Divvy Bike Rider Demographics, describing their Age, Gender, and how many are regular subscribers or one-time customers. If the user selectes the "Gender" button-icon, the total number of gender related statistics are presented as bar charts.

Selecting the "Age" button-icon displays a line graph containing containing information related to the number of trips by age-demographic.

Finally, the User can select the "User-Type" button-icon to see a bar chart detailing comparing the number of paying subscribers of the Divvy service compared to one time use customers.

Number of bikes by day of week,
hour of day, and day of year

The next set of charts (top-right) examines the temporal aspects of the Divvy bike data by comparing the peak day of the week in terms of number of bikes, the most active hour of the day (and night), and the most active day of the year.

By selecting the "Day of week" button-icon, the user is presented with a bar-chart which displays the number of bikes out total for each day of the week.

Selecting the "Hours of day" button-icon displays a line graph of the peak hours of the day in which the most bikes were taken out. The time scale ranges from 03am to 09pm in increments of three hours along the x axis.

Distribution of rides by distance, time,
and distance traveled for each bike

Each type of distribution can be examined by simply selecting the appropriate button-icon. When selecting the "Trip Distance" button, the User is presented with a line graph of the distribution of trips, or rides, by the distance traveled.

To view the distribution of time, select the "Trip time" button-icon. This view displays a bar chart with the duration of trips on the x axis and the number of trips as the y axis.

Play A Day

The "Play A Day" section of the application allows for a minute-by-minute view of Divvy bike usage across Chicago. To switch to this mode, select "Play A Day" from the bottom menu. Several features are available:

  • By clicking the calendar icon, you can select a specific month and day.
  • A specific time of day can be chosen by increasing or decreasing hours and minutes, and selecting "AM" or "PM."
  • Click the play button to begin animated Divvy bike usage for the selected date. Divvy bikes are displayed as triangles that move across the map from their departure station to their arrival station.
  • Divvy bikes will be shown travelling between selected stations. Initially, all stations will be selected. You may select individual stations by first clicking "None," to clear the selection, and then clicking on particular stations of interest.
  • After selecting a station, you may click "Community" to select all stations in the most recently selected station's community.
  • For a given time of day, the a sun/moon icon to the right of the current time will indicate when the sun was rising or setting for a given date and time.
  • A box towards the upper right displays the weather conditions for the selected date and time.
  • To the right, a chart will continuously update as the day progresses, showing the number of active Divvy bikes at each time of day. At any time, you may select several filters – below the chart – to filter the Divvy bike data. Filtering the data will also change the color of active bikes shown on the map, with filtered bikes shown in blue.

Community Layer

To see the boundaries of the iconic Chicagoan communities, click on the map boundary button under the "Overall" view. The boundaries are highlighted in a red, dashed line so that they stand out to the viewer, without occluding ones view of the rest of the map. Each community area conveniently carries a label with the community name centered within the communities boundary, so it is easily identified from its neighbors. To turn off the community areas, simply click on the map boundary button again.

Community Area Selection

To select an entire community area, first select a station in a region of interest. Then, select the COMMUNITY button-icon near the bottom menu. You will notice immediately that all stations within that community are selected. To add additional community areas, simply select a station in a community area of interest, and click the COMMUNITY button-icon again.
To deselect a community area, deselect a station within a selected region, and (you guessed it) select the COMMUNITY button-icon. This will result in all the stations within the community area being deselected.
To select All community areas, click on the ALL button-icon near the bottom menu. This will select every station in every community.
To deselect all community areas, click on the NONE button-icon near the bottom menu. This will remove all selected stations.

Individual Station Inflow and Outflow

To examine the Individual Station Inflow and Outflow, select the "Compare" button-icon on the bottom menu bar. Grey, circular icons will appear where the stations are located on the map. When the user selects a station, it will turn orange to indicate that it has been selected, and a series of blue lines will appear extending from the selected station to stations surrounding it. If the User selects "outflow" from the popup menu, a series of red lines will appear extending from the selected station to stations surrounding it on top of the blue lines. When the User selects "inflow" the blue lines are removed, showing only the red outflow lines. The User can turn the inflow and outflow lines on and off in any combination they please.

Inflow and outflow for two stations

In this same "Compare" view, any pair of stations can be selected by clicking on each station. When exactly two stations are selected, the flow balance between these two stations is shown with two arrows. The width of each arrow corresponds to the relative flow of bikes to and from each station.

When two stations are selected, a line chart is also displayed, showing the number of rides between the two selected stations by time of day.

Inflow and Outflow Differences

By selecting "Flow Balance" from the bottom menu, you may view the inflow and outflow difference for each station. Inflow and outflow are measured as the number of Divvy bikes entering or exiting a station per hour.

  • Stations are colored according to their relative inflow and outflow – stations with high outflow are red, and stations with high inflow are blue.
  • A specific station's inflow and outflow can be seen by double-clicking on that station.

Patterns Based on the Time of Day

To view patterns of movement based on the time of day, the user simply selects the "Patterns" button-icon on the bottom menu. Immediately a second menu appears listing typical periods of movement throughout the day. For example, to see what the morning commute around the city looks like, select "Morning".

Multiple Maps

At any time, you may bring up an additional map interface by selecting the circle near the bottom-left corner of the overview map. For each map, a bounding box is shown, and each box is colored green or orange, which corresponds to the color of the bar at the top of each map display.

Each map contains a fully independent interface, and any of the views described above can be accessed within each map.
